Let's Go Deeper Into The Analysis

If you frequently use Facebook and Instagram, you’ve likely encountered election ads from various political parties, notably BJP and INC. Political parties in India typically allocate significant funds for their election campaigns. Recently, I gathered data from Meta ads detailing the expenditure of each party on Instagram and Facebook during the 2024 Indian elections across different states. This article presents an analysis of election ad spending through Python and explores its influence on voting behaviors.

Top 5 Parties by Ad Spend

Insight: The top 5 parties contributed to 70% of total ad spend, influencing voter engagement significantly.

State-wise Spending (INR)

Insight: States with higher ad spend showed a direct correlation with higher voter turnout rates.

Voter Turnout by State

Insight: States with a higher voter turnout were generally those with more political activity and advertising.

Relationship Between Ad Spend and Voter Turnout

Insight: There’s a clear positive relationship between the amount spent on ads and the increase in voter turnout.

Top 5 Parties by Ad Spend (Pie Chart)

Insight: The BJP occupies the largest portion of ad spending, reflecting their substantial investment in outreach, while smaller parties spend significantly less.

Correlation Matrix

Insight: The correlation between ad spending and voter turnout is weak, suggesting other factors play a larger role in influencing voter behavior.

Conclusion

Despite significant investments in advertising, our analysis suggests that higher ad spending alone is not a reliable predictor of increased voter turnout. A multitude of factors, including campaign strategies, candidate appeal, and socio-political conditions, also play crucial roles in influencing voter engagement. While larger states often allocate more resources to advertising, this does not necessarily correlate with higher voter participation rates. Even major political parties, such as the BJP, may find their advertising efforts less impactful than anticipated in driving voter turnout..
